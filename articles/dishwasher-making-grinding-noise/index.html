Dishwasher Making Grinding Noise

Dishwasher making grinding noise? Start by checking for hard debris, spray arm interference, and filter-area buildup before you assume a pump problem.

Fast answer

Quick answer

When a dishwasher is making a grinding noise, the safest first checks are hard debris in the filter area, a spray arm clipping a tall item, or food particles circulating near the sump. If the sound remains after cleaning the accessible wash area and correcting the load, the cause is more likely inside the pump or motor system and needs professional attention.

Noise timing

When the grinding sound happens matters

  • If the sound appears only during spray action, check for spray-arm interference, loose utensils, or debris moving around the filter well.
  • If the sound starts during drain-out, the issue may be closer to debris in the drain path or pump area.
  • If the noise runs through most of the cycle and keeps returning after cleaning, the odds of a pump or motor problem rise sharply.
  • If the grinding is joined by burning smell, leaking, or interrupted operation, stop using the machine until it is checked.

Fast triage

Rule out easy causes before you worry about the pump

Many loud dishwasher sounds come from debris, labels, glass fragments, or spray arms striking cookware. That is good news because those causes can often be found without teardown if you slow down and inspect the accessible wash area carefully.

  • Clean the filter and compare the next cycle instead of repeating noisy runs back to back.
  • Reposition tall items and confirm both spray arms spin freely before testing again.
  • Use how to clean dishwasher filter and how to clean dishwasher spray arms if you need a deeper maintenance reset before deciding the sound is mechanical.

Avoidable issues

Avoid these mistakes

  • Running repeated cycles with a loud grinding sound without first checking for obvious debris or spray-arm interference.
  • Reaching into the sump area carelessly instead of following the manual and only performing routine-access cleaning.
  • Assuming every grinding noise means a motor failure before checking for broken glass, labels, or seeds near the filter.

Get set first

Before you start

Try to notice when the sound happens. A noise that appears only during spraying may point to arm interference or loose debris, while a persistent sound through multiple phases can suggest a deeper mechanical problem.

Step-by-step guidance

Safe checks before you test the machine again

  1. Turn the dishwasher off and let moving parts stop completely before inspecting the bottom of the tub.
  2. Remove and inspect the filter area for hard debris, labels, bone fragments, or glass pieces that the manual says you can access safely.
  3. Spin the spray arms by hand and check whether they strike utensils, trays, or tall lower-rack items.
  4. Look for anything loose that could rattle or grind during wash action, including small caps or fragments from damaged dishes.

Step-by-step guidance

Step-by-step routine for a grinding dishwasher sound

  1. Clean the filter and the surrounding accessible well so trapped particles are less likely to recirculate during the next cycle.
  2. Remove or reposition any item that interferes with spray-arm movement.
  3. Run a short empty rinse or a normal cycle, if appropriate for your model, to see whether the sound changes after debris removal.
  4. If the sound is milder, monitor the next full load with careful loading and a clean filter before making bigger assumptions.
  5. If the sound is still sharp, repeated, or worsening, stop using the dishwasher until it can be evaluated further.

Know when to stop

When to stop and call a professional

  • The grinding noise remains after you clean the accessible filter area and confirm spray arms are not hitting dishes.
  • You notice burning smells, leaks, intermittent operation, or obvious damage near the pump area.
  • The next step would require opening sealed pump or motor assemblies not intended for routine maintenance.

Know when to stop

When this is beyond routine maintenance

A grinding noise becomes a repair-level problem when safe debris removal and loading adjustments do not help, especially if the pump impeller, motor bearings, or other internal wash components may be involved.

Common questions

FAQ

  • Can a dirty filter make a dishwasher sound like it is grinding? Sometimes yes. Hard debris near the filter and sump can create rough noises as water moves it around.
  • Is it safe to keep using a noisy dishwasher? Not if the sound is strong, repeated, or getting worse. Continued use can make some mechanical problems more expensive.
  • Can dishes loaded too high cause strange noise? Yes. A spray arm striking a utensil or dish can sound harsher than many people expect.
