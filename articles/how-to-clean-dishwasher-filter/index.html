Fast answer

Quick answer

Dishwasher filter cleaning is usually routine maintenance: remove food debris, rinse with warm water, brush off film gently, and reinstall the filter exactly as the manual shows.

What you will need

Avoidable issues

Avoid these mistakes

  • Using a sharp pick that tears mesh or cracks plastic.
  • Reinstalling the filter loosely after cleaning.
  • Skipping the filter well where trapped debris causes odor and drainage issues.

Get set first

Before you start

Check the owner manual, let the dishwasher cool if it just finished a cycle, and stay within routine cleaning steps that do not require electrical or plumbing teardown.

Step-by-step guidance

Before you start cleaning the dishwasher filter

Check the owner's manual before removing anything, let the dishwasher cool, and gather a towel, warm water, and a soft brush. Most filter cleaning jobs are simple routine maintenance, but forcing a stuck twist-lock filter or using sharp tools can damage plastic parts. If the filter does not lift out the way the manual describes, stop there and avoid turning a cleaning task into a repair problem.

Step-by-step guidance

Step-by-step dishwasher filter cleaning routine

Remove the lower rack if needed, unlock the filter the way the manual shows, and rinse away loose debris with warm water. Use a soft brush to loosen film from the mesh and frame, then wipe the filter well before reinstalling it securely. While the filter is out, wipe the filter well and inspect for food scraps that could cause odor or slow drainage. Finish by running an empty maintenance cycle only if the manufacturer allows it.

Step-by-step guidance

When to stop and call a professional

Call a professional if the filter housing is cracked, the filter will not lock back into place, water backs up even after cleaning, or the machine shows drain errors. Those signs suggest the problem may be beyond routine maintenance. A cleaning article should help you stop early, not persuade you to force damaged or stuck parts.

Know when to stop

When this is beyond routine maintenance

  • The filter will not unlock or re-seat normally.
  • The housing is cracked or water backs up after cleaning.
  • Drain errors or pooled water remain after a full test cycle.

Common questions

FAQ

  • How often should I handle how to clean dishwasher filter? For many households, checking it weekly and cleaning it about once a month is a sensible starting routine, but the owner's manual and your soil load should guide the final schedule.
  • Can I soak a dishwasher filter in warm soapy water? Usually yes, if the manufacturer allows it: warm soapy water and a soft brush are safer than harsh scrubbing or strong chemical mixes.
  • When should I call a professional for how to clean dishwasher filter? Call when the next step would require repair-style disassembly, or when cleaning and user-safe checks do not change the symptom after a full cycle or two.
